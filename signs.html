Signs and Symptoms of M-Pox

Common Symptoms

M-Pox symptoms typically appear within 5 to 21 days after exposure. The most common symptoms include:

Fever and Chills: High fever, often with sudden chills.
Swollen Lymph Nodes: Painful swelling in the neck, armpits, or groin.
Extreme Fatigue: Severe tiredness, making daily tasks difficult.
Skin Rash: Rash that develops into fluid-filled blisters.
Headache and Muscle Aches: Persistent pain in the head and body.

Progression of Symptoms

  • Days 1-5: Fever, muscle aches, swollen glands.
  • Days 5+: Rash appears, spreading across the body.
  • Recovery: Rash dries and scabs over within 2-4 weeks.

When to Seek Medical Help

  • Fever that does not subside.
  • Severe rash spreading quickly.
  • Difficulty breathing or chest pain.

Preventive Measures

  • Get vaccinated if at risk.
  • Practice good hygiene and wash hands frequently.
  • Avoid contact with infected individuals.
  • Use protective equipment if handling infected materials.
