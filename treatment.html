Treatment of M-Pox

Managing M-Pox Symptoms

Currently, there is no specific cure for M-Pox, but symptoms can be managed with proper care. The following treatments can help:

  • Fever Management: Over-the-counter pain relievers such as acetaminophen (paracetamol) or ibuprofen can reduce fever and discomfort.
  • Skin Care: Keep the skin clean and dry. Avoid scratching rashes to prevent infection.
  • Hydration: Drink plenty of fluids to stay hydrated, especially if experiencing fever or diarrhea.
  • Rest and Isolation: Patients should get adequate rest and isolate to prevent spreading the virus.

When to Seek Medical Attention

If symptoms become severe or complications arise, seek medical care immediately. This includes:

  • Persistent high fever (above 102°F / 39°C).
  • Difficulty breathing or chest pain.
  • Severe dehydration (dizziness, confusion, no urination).
  • Skin infections (swelling, pus, worsening rashes).

Medical Treatments Available

Doctors may prescribe antiviral medications in severe cases, such as Tecovirimat (TPOXX), which has shown effectiveness in treating M-Pox.

It is crucial to follow professional medical advice and adhere to prescribed treatments for faster recovery.
